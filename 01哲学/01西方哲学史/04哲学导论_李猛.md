## MEDITATION II

### 5.

who desires to know more of them, and does not wish to be deceived;

他想象许多事情，有时甚至不顾自己的意愿;

who imagines many things, sometimes even despite his will;

同样，它也能感知许多事物，就好像通过感官的媒介一样。

and is likewise percipient of many, as if through the medium of the senses.

在这一切中，难道没有什么比我更真实的了吗?即使我总是在做梦，尽管给我生命的上帝用他所有的聪明才智来欺骗我。

**Is there nothing of all this as true as that I am, even although I should be always dreaming, and although he who gave me being employed all his ingenuity to deceive me?**

在这些属性中，是否也有任何一种可以恰当地与我的思想区分开来，或者可以说是与我自己分开的呢?

**Is there also any one of these attributes that can be properly distinguished from my thought, or that can be said to be separate from myself?**

因为它本身是如此明显，是我在怀疑，是我在理解，是我在渴望，所以在这里没有必要为了使它更清楚而添加任何东西。

**For it is of itself so evident that it is I who doubt, I who understand, and I who desire, that it is here unnecessary to add anything by way of rendering it more clear**.

> rendering 表达、解释

我当然也是那个想象的存在;

**And I am as certainly the same being who imagines;**

因为尽管我所想象的一切都可能是不真实的(正如我先前所设想的那样)，但想象的力量仍然存在于我的内心，并成为我思想的一部分。

**for although it may be (as I before supposed) that nothing I imagine is true, still the power of imagination does not cease really to exist in me and to form part of my thought.**

总之，我还是那个能感知的人，也就是说，我是通过感觉器官来理解某些事物的人，因为，事实上，我看到了光，听到了声音，感觉到了热。

**In fine, I am the same being who perceives, that is, who apprehends certain objects as by the organs of sense, since, in truth, I see light, hear a noise, and feel heat.**

但有人会说，这些表象都是假的，我是在做梦。

**But it will be said that these presentations are false, and that I am dreaming.**

就让它这样吧。

**Let it be so.**

无论如何，可以肯定的是，我似乎看到了光，听到了声音，感觉到了热;

**At all events it is certain that I seem to see light, hear a noise, and feel heat;**

这不可能是假的，这就是在我身上被恰当地称为感知的东西，它无非是思考。

**this cannot be false, and this is what in me is properly called perceiving (sentire), which is nothing else than thinking.**





------

### 10.

10.

由此，我开始比以前更清楚、更清楚地知道我是什么。

From this I begin to know what I am with somewhat greater clearness and distinctness than heretofore.

但是，尽管如此，在我看来，而且我不能不相信，有形的事物，其形象是由属于感觉之下的思想所构成的，并由感觉所检验的事物，是比我不知道我自己的哪一部分是不可想象的更清楚得多的;

But, nevertheless, it still seems to me, and I cannot help believing, that corporeal things, whose images are formed by thought which fall under the senses, and are examined by the same, are known with much greater distinctness than that I know not what part of myself which is not imaginable;

虽然，说实话，说我对那些我认为存在是可疑的、未知的、不属于我的事物，比我确信它们的存在是我所知道的、属于我固有本性的事物更清楚地了解和理解，这似乎是奇怪的;

although, in truth, it may seem strange to say that I know and comprehend with greater distinctness things whose existence appears to me doubtful, that are unknown, and do not belong to me, than others of whose reality I am persuaded, that are known to me, and appertain to my proper nature;

总之，比我自己。

in a word, than myself.

但我清楚地看到了情况。

But I see clearly what is the state of the case.

我的思想很容易走神，而且还不愿意被限制在真理的范围之内。

My mind is apt to wander, and will not yet submit to be restrained within the limits of truth.

因此，让我们再一次把精神留给它自己，让它有各种各样的自由去考虑从外部呈现给它的对象，以便在随后把它从这些对象中温和而适当地抽出来，把它集中在考虑它的存在和它在自身中发现的性质上，这样，精神就会更容易得到控制。

Let us therefore leave the mind to itself once more, and, according to it every kind of liberty permit it to consider the objects that appear to it from without, in order that, having afterward withdrawn it from these gently and opportunely and fixed it on the consideration of its being and the properties it finds in itself, it may then be the more easily controlled.

 

------

### 11.

11.

现在让我们据此来考虑那些通常被认为是最容易认识的，同样也是最清楚知道的物体，即我们触摸和看到的物体，实际上不是一般的物体，因为这些一般的概念通常比较混乱，而是一个特定的物体。

Let us now accordingly consider the objects that are commonly thought to be the most easily, and likewise the most distinctly known, viz, the bodies we touch and see, not, indeed, bodies in general, for these general notions are usually somewhat more confused, but one body in particular.

以这片蜡为例;

Take, for example, this piece of wax;

它很新鲜，刚从蜂巢里取出来;

it is quite fresh, having been but recently taken from the beehive;

它还没有失去它所含蜂蜜的甜味;

it has not yet lost the sweetness of the honey it contained;

它还保留着采集它的花朵的某种气味;

it still retains somewhat of the odor of the flowers from which it was gathered;

它的颜色、形状、大小都是显而易见的;

its color, figure, size, are apparent (to the sight);

它硬、冷、易处理;

it is hard, cold, easily handled;

用手指敲击时发出声音。

and sounds when struck upon with the finger.

总之，所有有助于使一个身体尽可能清楚地为人所知的东西，都在我们面前的这个身体中找到了。

In fine, all that contributes to make a body as distinctly known as possible, is found in the one before us.

但是，当我说话的时候，把它放在火旁边——剩下的味道被呼出来了，气味蒸发了，颜色改变了，它的形状被破坏了，它的大小变大了，它变成了液体，它变得热了，它几乎不能处理，虽然敲击它，但它不发出声音。

But, while I am speaking, let it be placed near the fire--what remained of the taste exhales, the smell evaporates, the color changes, its figure is destroyed, its size increases, it becomes liquid, it grows hot, it can hardly be handled, and, although struck upon, it emits no sound.

更换后的蜡还能保持原样吗?

Does the same wax still remain after this change?

必须承认，它仍然存在;

It must be admitted that it does remain;

没有人怀疑它，也没有人做出不同的判断。

no one doubts it, or judges otherwise.

那么，我在那块蜡上如此清楚地知道的是什么呢?

What, then, was it I knew with so much distinctness in the piece of wax?

当然，我用感官所观察到的一切都不可能是什么，因为所有属于味觉、嗅觉、视觉、触觉和听觉的东西都发生了变化，但蜡仍然是相同的。

Assuredly, it could be nothing of all that I observed by means of the senses, since all thel things that fell under taste, smell, sight, touch, and hearing are changed, and yet the same wax remains.



------

### 12.

12.

也许这就是我现在所想的，即，这种蜡既不是蜂蜜的甜味，也不是花朵的芳香，也不是白色，也不是声音，而只是一个身体，在我看来，在这些形式下，它是显而易见的，而在其他形式下，它是显而易见的。

It was perhaps what I now think, viz, that this wax was neither the sweetness of honey, the pleasant odor of flowers, the whiteness, the figure, nor the sound, but only a body that a little before appeared to me conspicuous under these forms, and which is now perceived under others.

但是，准确地说，当我这样想的时候，我的想象是什么呢?

But, to speak precisely, what is it that I imagine when I think of it in this17way?

让我们仔细地考虑一下，把所有不属于蜡的东西都去掉，让我们看看还剩下什么。

Let it be attentively considered, and, retrenching all that does not belong to the wax, let us see what remains.

当然，除了一些延伸的、灵活的、可移动的东西之外，什么也没有留下。

There certainly remains nothing, except something extended, flexible, and movable.

但是什么是灵活和可移动呢?

But what is meant by flexible and movable?

这不是我想象的吗。

Is it not that I imagine.

蜡是圆的，能变成方的，或者能从方变成三角形?

that the piece of wax, being round, is capable of becoming square, or of passing from a square into a triangular figure?

当然，情况并非如此，因为我认为，它允许无限的类似变化;

Assuredly such is not the case, because I conceive that it admits of an infinity of similar changes;

而且，我无法通过想象来跨越这个无限，因此，我对蜡的这个概念并不是想象力的产物。

and I am, moreover, unable to compass this infinity by imagination, and consequently this conception which I have of the wax is not the product of the faculty of imagination.

但现在这个扩展是什么?

But what now is this extension?

它不也是未知的吗?

Is it not also unknown?

因为蜡熔化的时候更大，烧开的时候更大，加热的时候更大。

for it becomes greater when the wax is melted, greater when it is boiled, and greater still when the heat increases;

如果我不认为我们所考虑的这片蜡具有比我所想象的更广泛的范围，那么我就不会清楚地、根据真理地想象它是什么，因此，我必须承认，我甚至不能通过想象来理解这片蜡是什么，它仅仅是心灵(男人，拉特。

and I should not conceive clearly and according to truth, the wax as it is, if I did not suppose that the piece we are considering admitted even of a wider variety of extension than I ever imagined, I must, therefore, admit that I cannot even comprehend by imagination what the piece of wax is, and that it is the mind alone (mens, Lat.

， entendement, f)感知它。

, entendement, F.) which perceives it.

我特别说的是一件，因为就一般的蜡而言，这一点更为明显。

I speak of one piece in particular, for as to wax in general, this is still more evident.

但是，那块只有理智或头脑才能感知的蜡是什么呢?

But what is the piece of wax that can be perceived only by the understanding or mind?

它当然是我所看到的、触摸到的、想象到的;

It is certainly the same which I see, touch, imagine;

总之，它和我一开始所相信的是一样的。

and, in fine, it is the same which, from the beginning, I believed it to be.

但(这是时刻观察)的感知它既不是一种视觉,触觉,也没有想象力,而且从不是这两种,虽然它似乎以前如此,但只是一种直觉(监测),这可能是不完美的和困惑,因为它曾经是,或非常清晰和明显的,因为它是目前,根据人们或多或少注意到它所包含的元素,它是由。

But (and this it is of moment to observe) the perception of it is neither an act of sight, of touch, nor of imagination, and never was either of these, though it might formerly seem so, but is simply an intuition (inspectio) of the mind, which may be imperfect and confused, as it formerly was, or very clear and distinct, as it is at present, according as the attention is more or less directed to the elements which it contains, and of which it is composed.

 

------

### 13.

13.

但是，与此同时，当我观察到自己思想的弱点和容易出错的倾向时，我感到非常惊讶。

But, meanwhile, I feel greatly astonished when I observe the weakness of my mind, and its proneness to error.

因为，尽管我根本没有把我所想的表达出来，我在自己的头脑中考虑了这一切，但言语偶尔会阻碍我的进步，我几乎会被普通语言的术语引入错误。

For although, without at all giving expression to what I think, I consider all this in my own mind, words yet occasionally impede my progress, and I am almost led into error by the terms of ordinary language.

我们说,例如,我们看到同样的蜡在我们面前时,并不是我们判断它是相同的从其保留相同的颜色和图:那里我应该立即被处理得出的蜡是已知的,而不是单靠心灵的直觉,如果不是人类的类似的实例传递在大街上,观察到从一个窗口。

We say, for example, that we see the same wax when it is before us, and not that we judge it to be the same from its retaining the same color and figure: whence I should forthwith be disposed to conclude that the wax is known by the act of sight, and not by the intuition of the mind alone, were it not for the analogous instance of human beings passing on in the street below, as observed from a window.

在这种情况下，我可以说我看到了这些人本身，就像我说我看到了蜡一样;

In this case I do not fail to say that I see the men themselves, just as I say that I see the wax;

然而，我从窗口看到的是什么，除了帽子和斗篷之外，这些帽子和斗篷可能覆盖着人工机器，它们的运动可能由弹簧决定?

and yet what do I see from the window beyond hats and cloaks that might cover artificial machines, whose motions might be determined by springs?

但我从这些表象中判断出有人类存在，因此我仅凭心灵中的判断能力就理解了我相信我亲眼所见的东西。

But I judge that there are human beings from these appearances, and thus 1 comprehend, by the faculty of judgment alone which is in the mind, what I believed I saw with my eyes.

 

------

### 14.

14.

一个以提高自己的知识高于常人为目标的人，应该羞于从庸俗的言语形式中寻找怀疑的机会:

The man who makes it his aim to rise to knowledge superior to the common, ought to be ashamed to seek occasions of doubting from the vulgar forms of speech:

因此，与其这样做，我将着手处理手头的事情，问问自己，当我第一次看到那块蜡时，我是否对它有了更清晰、更完美的感知，当我认为我通过外部感觉本身，或者无论如何，通过所谓的常识(sensus communis)，也就是通过想象的能力来了解它的时候;

instead, therefore, of doing this, I shall proceed with the matter in hand, and inquire whether I had a clearer and more perfect perception of the piece of wax when I first saw it, and when I thought I knew it by means of the external sense itself, or, at all events, by the common sense (sensus communis), as it is called, that is, by the imaginative faculty;

或者，在我更加仔细地考察了它是什么，以及如何才能知道它之后，我现在是否更清楚地理解了它。

or whether I rather apprehend it more clearly at present, after having examined with greater care, both what it is, and in what way it can be known.

对这一点抱任何怀疑的态度当然是荒谬的。

It would certainly be ridiculous to entertain18any doubt on this point.

在最初的知觉中，有什么不同呢?

For what, in that first perception, was there distinct?

我察觉到了什么动物可能察觉不到的东西?

What did I perceive which any animal might not have perceived?

但是，当我把蜡和它的外表区别开来，当我把它的外衣剥掉，我认为它是赤裸裸的，可以肯定的是，尽管在我的判断中仍然可以发现一些错误，但是，如果没有人的头脑，我就不能这样理解它。

But when I distinguish the wax from its exterior forms, and when, as if I had stripped it of its vestments, I consider it quite naked, it is certain, although some error may still be found in my judgment, that I cannot, nevertheless, thus apprehend it without possessing a human mind.

 

------

### 15.

15.

但最后，关于心灵本身，也就是关于我自己，我该说些什么呢?

But finally, what shall I say of the mind itself, that is, of myself?

因为到目前为止，我还不承认我只是头脑。

For as yet I do not admit that I am anything but mind.

那么!

What, then!

我似乎对那块蜡有着如此清晰的理解，难道我不更真实、更肯定、更清楚、更清楚地认识自己吗?

I who seem to possess so distinct an apprehension of the piece of wax, do I not know myself, both with greater truth and certitude, and also much more distinctly and clearly?

因为，如果我判断蜡的存在是因为我看到了它，那么，更明显的是，我自己之所以存在或存在，也是出于同样的原因:因为我所看到的东西可能实际上并不是蜡，我甚至没有可以看到任何东西的眼睛;

For if I judge that the wax exists because I see it, it assuredly follows, much more evidently, that I myself am or exist, for the same reason: for it is possible that what I see may not in truth be wax, and that I do not even possess eyes with which to see anything;

但是当我看见的时候，或者同样地，当我认为我看见的时候，我自己认为我什么都不是，这是不可能的。

but it cannot be that when I see, or, which comes to the same thing, when I think I see, I myself who think am nothing.

同样地，如果我因为触摸了蜡而断定它是存在的，那么它仍然会得出我是存在的结论;

So likewise, if I judge that the wax exists because I touch it, it will still also follow that I am;

如果我确定，我的想象，或任何其他原因，无论是什么，说服我蜡的存在，我仍然会得出同样的结论。

and if I determine that my imagination, or any other cause, whatever it be, persuades me of the existence of the wax, I will still draw the same conclusion.

这里所说的那块蜡，也适用于我外在的一切事物。

And what is here remarked of the piece of wax, is applicable to all the other things that are external to me.

此外，如果蜡的概念或感知在我看来更加精确和清晰，在那之后，不仅是视觉和触觉，而且还有许多其他的原因，使我的理解变得更加清晰，那么我现在对自己的认识该是多么清晰啊，因为所有有助于认识蜡的性质或任何物体的性质的原因，都更清楚地表明了我的心灵的性质?

And further, if the notion or perception of wax appeared to me more precise and distinct, after that not only sight and touch, but many other causes besides, rendered it manifest to my apprehension, with how much greater distinctness must I now know myself, since all the reasons that contribute to the knowledge of the nature of wax, or of any body whatever, manifest still better the nature of my mind?

除此之外，精神本身还有许多其他事物有助于说明它的本性，我在这里所提到的那些依赖于肉体的事物，几乎是不值得考虑的。

And there are besides so many other things in the mind itself that contribute to the illustration of its nature, that those dependent on the body, to which I have here referred, scarcely merit to be taken into account.

 

------

### 16.

16.

但是，总而言之，我发现我不知不觉地回到了我所期望的地步;

But, in conclusion, I find I have insensibly reverted to the point I desired;

因为，既然现在对我来说很明显，物体本身不是由感官或想象力正确地感知的，而是由智力单独感知的;

for, since it is now manifest to me that bodies themselves are not properly perceived by the senses nor by the faculty of imagination, but by the intellect alone;

它们之所以被感知，并不是因为它们被看到和触摸，而是因为它们被思想理解或正确地理解，我很快发现，没有什么比我自己的心灵更容易或更清楚地理解了。

and since they are not perceived because they are seen and touched, but only because they are understood or rightly comprehended by thought, I readily discover that there is nothing more easily or clearly apprehended than my own mind.

但是，由于要如此迅速地摆脱一个人早已习惯了的观点是很困难的，因此，在这个阶段停留一段时间是可取的，这样，通过长期持续的冥想，我可以更深刻地铭记这一新知识。

But because it is difficult to rid one's self so promptly of an opinion to which one has been long accustomed, it will be desirable to tarry for some time at this stage, that, by long continued meditation, I may more deeply impress upon my memory this new knowledge.

## MEDITATION III 

OF GOD: THAT HE EXISTS

------
### 1.


1.

现在我要闭上眼睛，堵住耳朵，让我的感官远离事物，我甚至要把一切有形事物的形象从我的意识中抹去;


I WILL now close my eyes, I will stop my ears, I will turn away my senses from their objects, I will even efface from my consciousness all the images of corporeal things;

或者至少，因为这几乎不可能实现，我将认为它们是空洞和虚假的;


or at least, because this can hardly be accomplished, I will consider them as empty and false;

因此，我只与自己交谈，仔细审视自己的本性，努力逐步地对自己有更深入、更熟悉的认识。


and thus, holding converse only with myself, and closely examining my nature, I will endeavor to obtain by degrees a more intimate and familiar knowledge of myself.

我是一个有思想(有意识)的东西，也就是说，我是一个怀疑、肯定、否定、知道一些“对象”，而对许多对象却一无所知的存在——我爱、恨、意志、拒绝，我同样想象，我感知;


I am a thinking (conscious) thing, that is, a being who doubts, affirms, denies, knows a few " objects, and is ignorant of many,-- who loves, hates, wills, refuses, who imagines likewise, and perceives;

因为，正如我前面说过的，虽然我所感知或想象的事物，也许与我无关，而且就它们自身而言，我确信那些我称之为知觉和想象的意识方式，仅就它们是意识的方式而言，是存在于我之中的。


for, as I before remarked, although the things which I perceive or imagine are perhaps nothing at all apart from me and in themselves, I am nevertheless assured that those modes of consciousness which I call perceptions and imaginations, in as far only as they are modes of consciousness, exist in me.

------
### 2.


2.

在我所说的这几句话里，我想我已经总结了我真正知道的一切，或者至少是到目前为止我所知道的一切。


And in the little I have said I think I have summed up all that I really know, or at least all that up to this time I was aware I knew.

现在，当我努力更广泛地扩展我的知识时，我将谨慎地考虑我是否还能在自己身上进一步发现我迄今为止没有观察到的东西。


Now, as I am endeavoring to extend my knowledge more widely, I will use circumspection, and consider with care whether I can still discover in myself anything further which I have not yet hitherto observed.

我确信我是一个会思考的东西;


I am certain that I am a thinking thing;

但难道我不也同样知道怎样才能使我确信真理吗?


but do I not therefore likewise know what is required to render me certain of a truth?

毫无疑问，在这第一种知识中，除了对我所肯定的事物的清晰而清晰的感知之外，没有任何东西能使我确信它是真实的，这并不足以使我确信我所说的事物是真实的，如果我如此清晰而清晰地感知到的事物会被证明是错误的;


In this first knowledge, doubtless, there is nothing that gives me assurance of its truth except the clear and distinct perception of what I affirm, which would not indeed be sufficient to give me the assurance that what I say is true, if it could ever happen that anything I thus clearly and distinctly perceived should prove false;

因此，在我看来，我现在可以作为一个一般的规则，一切非常清楚和清楚地理解(设想)是真实的。


and accordingly it seems to me that I may now take as a general rule, that all that is very clearly and distinctly apprehended (conceived) is true.

------
### 3.


3.

然而，我以前所接受和承认的许多事情是完全肯定和明显的，但我后来发现是可疑的。


Nevertheless I before received and admitted many things as wholly certain and manifest, which yet I afterward found to be doubtful.

那么，这些是什么呢?


What, then, were those?

它们是大地、天空、星星和所有其他我习惯用感官感知的物体。


They were the earth, the sky, the stars, and all the other objects which I was in the habit of perceiving by the senses.

但是，我在他们身上清楚、清楚地看到了什么呢?


But what was it that I clearly and distinctly perceived in them?

只不过是那些事物的思想和观念呈现在我的脑海里。


Nothing more than that the ideas and the thoughts of those objects were presented to my mind.

即使到现在，我也不否认这些想法存在于我的脑海中。


And even now I do not deny that these ideas are found in my mind.

不过，还有一件事是我肯定的，由于我习惯于相信这件事，所以我认为我清楚地看到了，尽管事实上我根本没有看到。


But there was yet another thing which I affirmed, and which, from having been accustomed to believe it, I thought I clearly perceived, although, in truth, I did not perceive it at all;

我指的是存在于我之外的客体，这些观念是从这些客体中产生的，它们与这些客体有完全的相似之处;


I mean the existence of objects external to me, from which those ideas proceeded, and to which they had a perfect resemblance;

在这一点上我错了，或者如果我判断正确的话，这肯定不是我所拥有的任何知识(我的感知力，拉特)。


and it was here I was mistaken, or if I judged correctly, this assuredly was not to be traced to any knowledge I possessed (the force of my perception, Lat.).

### 4.


4.

但是，当我在算术和几何上考虑任何问题时，这是非常简单和容易的，例如，二加三等于五，以及诸如此类的事情，难道我不至少足够清楚地看待它们，以保证我肯定它们的真理吗?


But when I considered any matter in arithmetic and geometry, that was very simple and easy, as, for example, that two and three added together make five, and things of this sort, did I not view them with at least sufficient clearness to warrant me in affirming their truth?

事实上，如果我后来判断我们应该怀疑这些事情，那是因为我想到，也许是上帝赋予了我这样一种本性，使我即使在我看来最明显是真实的事情上也会被欺骗。


Indeed, if I afterward judged that we ought to doubt of these things, it was for no other reason than because it occurred to me that a God might perhaps have given me such20a nature as that I should be deceived, even respecting the matters that appeared to me the most evidently true.

但是，每当这种先入为主的上帝至高无上的权力的观点出现在我的脑海中时，我不得不承认，如果他愿意的话，他很容易使我出错，甚至在我认为我拥有最高证据的事情上;


But as often as this preconceived opinion of the sovereign power of a God presents itself to my mind, I am constrained to admit that it is easy for him, if he wishes it, to cause me to err, even in matters where I think I possess the highest evidenee;

另一方面，每当我把注意力集中在我认为自己理解得非常清楚的事物上时，我就非常相信它们是真实的，以至于我很自然地说出这样的话:


and, on the other hand, as often as I direct my attention to things which I think I apprehend with great clearness, I am so persuaded of their truth that I naturally break out into expressions such as these:

欺骗我吧，只要我意识到我不是，就没有人能够证明我不是，或者在未来的任何时候，使我从来没有是真的，现在我是真的，或者使两个和三个比五个多或少，在假设这一点和其他类似的荒谬之处，我发现了一个明显的矛盾。


Deceive me who may, no one will yet ever be able to bring it about that I am not, so long as I shall be conscious that I am, or at any future time cause it to be true that I have never been, it being now true that I am, or make two and three more or less than five, in supposing which, and other like absurdities, I discover a manifest contradiction.

事实上，由于我没有理由相信神是骗人的，而且，事实上，我甚至没有考虑过任何一种神的存在所依据的理由，因此，仅仅基于这种假设的怀疑是非常微弱的，可以说是形而上学的。


And in truth, as I have no ground for believing that Deity is deceitful, and as, indeed, I have not even considered the reasons by which the existence of a Deity of any kind is established, the ground of doubt that rests only on this supposition is very slight, and, so to speak, metaphysical.

但是，为了能够完全把它除去，我必须在一有这样做的机会的时候，就去打听是否有上帝存在;


But, that I may be able wholly to remove it, I must inquire whether there is a God, as soon as an opportunity of doing so shall present itself;

如果我发现有一个上帝，我也必须同样检查他是否会是一个骗子;


and if I find that there is a God, I must examine likewise whether he can be a deceiver;

因为，没有这两条真理的知识，我看不出我能确定任何事情。


for, without the knowledge of these two truths, I do not see that I can ever be certain of anything.

为了能够在不中断我自己提出的思考顺序的情况下检验这一点，即从我首先在脑海中发现的概念逐步过渡到我后来在其中发现的概念，在这个阶段有必要把我的所有思想分成若干类，并考虑严格地说，在这些类中可以发现真理和错误。


And that I may be enabled to examine this without interrupting the order of meditation I have proposed to myself which is, to pass by degrees from the notions that I shall find first in my mind to those I shall afterward discover in it, it is necessary at this stage to divide all my thoughts into certain classes, and to consider in which of these classes truth and error are, strictly speaking, to be found.

### 5.


5.

在我的思想中，有些好象是事物的表象，而只有这些表象才称得上是理念。


Of my thoughts some are, as it were, images of things, and to these alone properly belongs the name IDEA;

就像当我在脑海中想象一个人、一个幻想、天空、一个天使或上帝。


as when I think represent to my mind a man, a chimera, the sky, an angel or God.

另一些则有其他形式;


Others, again, have certain other forms;

就像当我愿意、害怕、肯定或否定时，我的确总是把某种东西当作我思想的对象，但我在思想中也包含着某种东西，而不仅仅是客体的表象;


as when I will, fear, affirm, or deny, I always, indeed, apprehend something as the object of my thought, but I also embrace in thought something more than the representation of the object;

在这一类思想中，有的被称为意志或情感，有的被称为判断。


and of this class of thoughts some are called volitions or affections, and others judgments.

------
### 6.


6.

至于理念，如果我们只考察理念的自身，而不涉及理念以外的任何对象，那么理念就不可能是虚假的;


Now, with respect to ideas, if these are considered only in themselves, and are not referred to any object beyond them, they cannot, properly speaking, be false;

因为，不管我想象的是山羊还是奇美拉，我想象的前者并不比后者少。


for, whether I imagine a goat or chimera, it is not less true that I imagine the one than the other.

我们也不必担心意志或感情中可能存在虚假;


Nor need we fear that falsity may exist in the will or affections;

因为，尽管我可能渴望错误的对象，甚至从未存在过的对象，但我渴望它们仍然是真实的。


for, although I may desire objects that are wrong, and even that never existed, it is still true that I desire them.

这样，只剩下我们的判断，我们必须谨慎谨慎，免得受迷惑。


There thus only remain our judgments, in which we must take diligent heed that we be not deceived.

但是，它们所产生的最主要和最常见的错误，在于判断我们内在的观念与我们外在的事物相似或一致;


But the chief and most ordinary error that arises in them consists in judging that the ideas which are in us are like or conformed to the things that are external to us;

因为，如果我们只把理念本身看作是我们思维(意识)的某些规定，而不把理念引到别的方面，理念就不会有任何错误的机会。


for assuredly, if we but considered the ideas themselves as certain modes of our thought (consciousness), without referring them to anything beyond, they would hardly afford any occasion of error.

### 7.


7.

但在这些观念中，我觉得有些是天生的，有些是外来的，还有一些是我自己创造的(人为的);


But among these ideas, some appear to me to be innate, others adventitious, and others to be made by myself (factitious);

因为，当我有能力构思所谓的事物、真理或思想时，在我看来，我拥有这种能力不是来自于我自己的本性，而是来自于其他的来源;


for, as I have the power of conceiving what is called a thing, or a truth, or a thought, it seems to me that I hold this power from no other source than my own nature;

但是，如果我现在听到一种声音，如果我看到太阳，或者如果我感到热，我始终判断，这些感觉来自于存在于我自身之外的某些对象;


but if I now hear a noise, if I see the sun, or if I feel heat, I have all21along judged that these sensations proceeded from certain objects existing out of myself;

总之，在我看来，海妖、鹰头兽之类的东西都是我自己头脑的发明。


and, in fine, it appears to me that sirens, hippogryphs, and the like, are inventions of my own mind.

但我甚至可能会认为，我所有的观念都属于我称之为外来的一类，或者它们都是天生的，或者它们都是人为的;


But I may even perhaps come to be of opinion that all my ideas are of the class which I call adventitious, or that they are all innate, or that they are all factitious;

因为我还没有清楚地发现它们真正的起源。


for I have not yet clearly discovered their true origin.

------
### 8.


8.

我在这里要做的主要是考虑，对于那些似乎来自于不由我存在的某些对象的事物，有什么根据可以把它们看作是这些对象。


What I have here principally to do is to consider, with reference to those that appear to come from certain objects without me, what grounds there are for thinking them like these objects.

第一个理由是，在我看来，我是天生如此;


The first of these grounds is that it seems to me I am so taught by nature;

第二，我意识到这些想法并不依赖于我的意志，因此也不依赖于我自己，因为它们经常违背我的意志出现在我面前，就像目前一样，不管我愿意与否，我感到很热;


and the second that I am conscious that those ideas are not dependent on my will, and therefore not on myself, for they are frequently presented to me against my will, as at present, whether I will or not, I feel heat;

因此，我相信这种热的感觉或观念(感觉和观念)是由某种不同于我自己的东西在我体内产生的，也就是说，由我所坐的火的热量产生的。


and I am thus persuaded that this sensation or idea (sensum vel ideam) of heat is produced in me by something different from myself, viz., by the heat of the fire by which I sit.

我们可以很合理地假设，这件物品给我的印象是它自身的相似，而不是其他任何东西。


And it is very reasonable to suppose that this object impresses me with its own likeness rather than any other thing.

### 9.


9.

但我必须考虑这些理由是否足够有力和令人信服。


But I must consider whether these reasons are sufficiently strong and convincing.

当我谈到在这个问题上受自然的教育时，我所理解的自然这个词只是一种自发的动力，它促使我相信观念和客体之间有相似之处，而不是一种自然光，它提供了关于真理的知识。


When I speak of being taught by nature in this matter, I understand by the word nature only a certain spontaneous impetus that impels me to believe in a resemblance between ideas and their ohjects, and not a natural light that affords a knowledge of its truth.

但这两件事有很大的不同;


But these two things are widely different;

因为自然光所显示的真理是绝对不可能被怀疑的，例如，我之所以是因为我怀疑，以及其他类似的真理;


for what the natural light shows to be true can be in no degree doubtful, as, for example, that I am because I doubt, and other truths of the like kind;

因为我没有其他辨别真伪的能力，这种能力可以教我辨别自然之光所宣称的真理的伪性，而自然之光又是同样值得信赖的;


inasmuch as I possess no other faculty whereby to distinguish truth from error, which can teach me the falsity of what the natural light declares to be true, and which is equally trustworthy;

但就那些看似自然的冲动而言，我观察到，当涉及到行动的对与错的选择时，它们常常使我选择最坏的那一方;


but with respect to seemingly natural impulses, I have observed, when the question related to the choice of right or wrong in action, that they frequently led me to take the worse part;

在真理与谬误的问题上，我也看不出有什么更好的理由去追随他们。


nor do I see that I have any better ground for following them in what relates to truth and error.

总之，虽然我承认它们是由这些对象产生的，但它们一定象这些对象并不是必然的结果。


And, in fine, although I should grant that they proceeded from those objects, it is not a necessary consequence that they must be like them.

相反，我在许多例子中观察到，在对象和它的思想之间有很大的不同。

Thus, for example, I find in my mind two wholly diverse ideas of the sun;

and reason teaches me that the one which seems to have immediately emanated from it is the most unlike.

### 12.


12.

这些事情充分证明，迄今为止，我相信存在着某些与我不同的事物，不是出于某种特定的、深思熟虑的判断，而是出于某种盲目的冲动，这些事物通过感觉器官或其他任何可能的方式，把它们的思想或形象传达到我的脑海中，并给我留下了它们的相似之处。


And these things sufficiently prove that hitherto it has not been from a certain and deliberate judgment, but only from a sort of blind impulse, that I believed existence of certain things different from myself, which, by the organs of sense, or by whatever other means it might be, conveyed their ideas or images into my mind and impressed it with their likenesses.

### 13.


13.

但是，还有另一种方法来考察那些在我头脑中有思想的对象中，是否有存在于我之外的东西。


But there is still another way of inquiring whether, of the objects whose ideas are in my mind, there are any that exist out of me.

如果把观念仅仅当作意识的某些形式来理解，那么我并没有指出它们之间有什么不同或不平等，而且所有的观念似乎都同样是从我自己出发的，但是，如果把它们看作意象，一个代表一种东西，另一个代表另一种东西，那么它们之间显然有很大的差异。


If ideas are taken in so far only as they are certain modes of consciousness, I do not remark any difference or inequality among them, and all seem, in the same manner, to proceed from myself, but, considering them as images, of which one represents one thing and another a different, it is evident that a great diversity obtains among them.

因为，毫无疑问，那些代表实体的东西比那些只代表模式或偶然性的东西更具有某种东西，可以说，它们本身包含着更客观的实在性，也就是说，通过表象，它们比那些只代表模式或偶然性的东西具有更高的存在或完美的程度;


For, without doubt, those that represent substances are something more, and contain in themselves, so to speak, more objective reality that is, participate by representation in higher degrees of being or perfection, than those that represent only modes or accidents;

再者，我认为上帝是至高无上的、永恒的、无限的、不变的、无所不知的、全能的，是一切超出他自身的事物的创造者，这种观念，我说，当然比那些代表有限物质的观念有更客观的现实性。


and again, the idea by which I conceive a God sovereign, eternal, infinite, immutable, all-knowing, all-powerful, and the creator of all things that are out of himself, this, I say, has certainly in it more objective reality than those ideas by which finite substances are represented.

### 14.


14.

从自然光看来，在有效的和完全的原因中，至少应当有与它的结果同样多的实在性;


Now, it is manifest by the natural light that there must at least be as much reality in the efficient and total cause as in its effect;

因为，如果结果不能从原因中得出它的实在性，那又从何而来呢?


for whence can the effect draw its reality if not from its cause?

而如果原因本身没有这种现实，它又怎么能把这种现实传达给它呢?


And how could the cause communicate to it this reality unless it possessed it in itself?

因此，不仅存在的东西不能由不存在的东西产生，而且更完美的东西，换句话说，它本身包含更多的实在性的东西，也不可能是较不完美的东西的结果;


And hence it follows, not only that what is cannot be produced by what is not, but likewise that the more perfect, in other words, that which contains in itself more reality, cannot be the effect of the less perfect;

这不仅明显地适用于那些现实的或形式的效果，而且同样适用于那些仅仅被认为是客观的现实的观念。


and this is not only evidently true of those effects, whose reality is actual or formal, but likewise of ideas, whose reality is only considered as objective.

因此，例如，一块还不存在的石头，不仅现在不能开始存在，除非它是由在形式上或显著地拥有其本身所组成的一切的东西产生的，换句话说，由在本身中包含与石头相同的特性或其他优于它们的特性的东西产生的;


Thus, for example, the stone that is not yet in existence, not only cannot now commence to be, unless it be produced by that which possesses in itself, formally or eminently, all that enters into its composition, in other words, by that which contains in itself the same properties that are in the stone, or others superior to them;

热只能在一个没有热的主体中产生，产生的原因在某种程度上或种类上至少和热一样完美;


and heat can only be produced in a subject that was before devoid of it, by a cause that is of an order, degree or kind, at least as perfect as heat;

其他人也是如此。


and so of the others.

但更进一步说，即使是热的概念或石头的概念，也不能存在于我里面，除非有一个原因把它放在我里面，这个原因至少包含了我认为存在于热或石头里的同样多的实在性，因为尽管这个原因不能把它的任何实际的或形式的实在性传递到我的观念里，但我们不应该因此想象它不那么实在;


But further, even the idea of the heat, or of the stone, cannot exist in me unless it be put there by a cause that contains, at least, as much reality as I conceive existent in the heat or in the stone for although that cause may not transmit into my idea anything of its actual or formal reality, we ought not on this account to imagine that it is less real;

但是，我们应当认识到，既然每一个观念都是心灵的产物，那么它的性质就在于它本身，除了它从我们的意识中借来的形式实在之外，不需要别的形式实在，它只是我们意识的一种形式，即一种思维方式或方式。


but we ought to consider that, as every idea is a work of the mind, its nature is such as of itself to demand no other formal reality than that which it borrows from our consciousness, of which it is but a mode that is, a manner or way of thinking.

但是，为了使理念包含这种客观实在而不是那种客观实在，毫无疑问，它必须从某种原因中推导出这种客观实在，而在这种原因中，至少可以发现形式实在与理念所包含的客观实在一样多;


But in order that an idea may contain this objective reality rather than that, it must doubtless derive it from some cause in which is found at least as much formal reality as the idea contains of objective;

因为，如果我们假定在一个理念中可以找到不属于它的原因的东西，那它当然是无中生有的。


for, if we suppose that there is found in an idea anything which was not in its cause, it must of course derive this from nothing.

但是，无论一个事物在客观地或在表象上被它的理念所理解的存在方式是多么不完美，我们当然不能因此说这种存在方式是虚无的，也不能因此说理念的起源是虚无的。


But, however imperfect may be the mode of existence by23which a thing is objectively or by representation in the understanding by its idea, we certainly cannot, for all that, allege that this mode of existence is nothing, nor, consequently, that the idea owes its origin to nothing.

### 15.

因为，正相反，很明显，我，一个有思想的存在，要从无到有，要获得许多我所不知道的事物的知识，而这些事物仅仅是一个有思想的实体的偶然事件，要困难得多;


for, on the contrary, it is quite manifest that it was a matter of much higher difficulty that I, a thinking being, should arise from nothing, than it would be for me to acquire the knowledge of many things of which I am ignorant, and which are merely the accidents of a thinking substance;

当然，如果我拥有我现在所说的更完美的自我，换句话说，如果我是我自己存在的作者，我至少不会否认自己可能更容易获得的东西，就像我目前所缺乏的无限多样的知识一样。


and certainly, if I possessed of myself the greater perfection of which I have now spoken in other words, if I were the author of my own existence, I would not at least have denied to myself things that may be more easily obtained as that infinite variety of knowledge of which I am at present destitute.

事实上，我不能否认我所觉察到的包含在上帝观念中的任何属性，因为在我看来，这些属性中没有比这更难以创造或获得的了;


I could not, indeed, have denied to myself any property which I perceive is contained in the idea of God, because there is none of these that seems to me to be more difficult to make or acquire;

如果有什么东西碰巧更难获得，它们对我来说肯定是如此(假设我自己是我所拥有的其他东西的来源)，因为我会发现它们对我的能力有一个限制。


and if there were any that should happen to be more difficult to acquire, they would certainly appear so to me (supposing that I myself were the source of the other things I possess), because I should discover in them a limit to my power.

------
### 31.


31.

虽然我假定我总是像现在这样，但基于这个理由，我不应该逃避这些推理的力量，因为即使基于这个假设，也不可能得出我的存在没有作者需要寻找的结论。


And though I were to suppose that I always was as I now am, I should not, on this ground, escape the force of these reasonings, since it would not follow, even on this supposition, that no author of my existence needed to be sought after.

因为我生命的整个时间可能被分成无数个部分，每一个部分都不依赖于任何其他部分;


For the whole time of my life may be divided into an infinity of parts, each of which is in no way dependent on any other;

因此，因为我是在不久以前存在的，这并不意味着我现在必须存在，除非在这一刻有某种原因重新创造了我，也就是说，保存了我。


and, accordingly, because I was in existence a short time ago, it does not follow that I must now exist, unless in this moment some cause create me anew as it were,that is, conserve me.

事实上，对于所有会仔细考虑持续时间本质的人来说，这是非常清楚和明显的，在其持续时间的每一刻，一种物质的保存需要相同的力量和行为，这将是创造它所必需的，假设它还不存在;


In truth, it is perfectly clear and evident to all who will attentively consider the nature of duration, that the conservation of a substance, in each moment of its duration, requires the same power and act that would be necessary to create it, supposing it were not yet in existence;

因此，自然之光明显地规定，守恒和创造的区别仅仅在于我们的思维方式，而不是在现实中。


so that it is manifestly a dictate of the natural light that conservation and creation differ merely in respect of our mode of thinking and not in reality.

------
### 32.


32.

因此，这里所要求的就是，我自问自己是否拥有某种力量，通过这种力量，我可以使现在的我在以后的某一时刻存在:因为，既然我只是一个思考的东西(或者，至少，在此期间，确切的问题只是我自己的那一部分)，如果这样一种力量存在于我体内，毫无疑问，我应该意识到它;


All that is here required, therefore, is that I interrogate myself to discover whether I possess any power by means of which I can bring it about that I, who now am, shall exist a moment afterward: for, since I am merely a thinking thing (or since, at least, the precise question, in the meantime, is only of that part of myself), if such a power resided in me, I should, without doubt, be conscious of it;

但我没有意识到这种力量，因此我清楚地知道，我依赖于某种与我不同的存在。


but I am conscious of no such power, and thereby I manifestly know that I am dependent upon some being different from myself.

------
### 33.


33.

但是，也许我所依赖的存在并不是上帝，我是由我的父母或由一些不如上帝完美的原因产生的。


But perhaps the being upon whom I am dependent is not God, and I have been produced either by my parents, or by some causes less perfect than Deity.

这是不可能的，因为，正如我前面说过的，很显然，在原因中至少必须有与结果同样多的真实性;


This cannot be: for, as I before said, it is perfectly evident that there must at least be as much reality in the cause as in its effect;

因此，既然我是一个会思考的东西，并且在我自己里面有一个关于上帝的观念，那么，无论我存在的原因到底是什么，我们都必须承认，它同样也是一个会思考的存在，而且它本身也有我赋予神的观念和一切完美。


and accordingly, since l am a thinking thing and possess in myself an idea of God, whatever in the end be the cause of my existence, it must of necessity be admitted that it is likewise a thinking being, and that it possesses in itself the idea and all the perfections I attribute to Deity.

那么，我们又可以追问这个原因的起源和存在是由于它自己，还是由于别的原因。


Then it may again be inquired whether this cause owes its origin and existence to itself, or to some other cause.

因为如果它是自我存在的，那么根据我前面所指出的，这原因就是上帝;


For if it be selfexistent, it follows, from what I have before laid down, that this cause is God;

因为，既然它具有自我存在的完满性，那么，毫无疑问，它也必然具有一种力量，即实际上具有它所认为的一切完满性——换句话说，它具有我认为属于上帝的一切完满性。


for, since it possesses the perfection of self-existence, it must likewise, without doubt, have the power of actually possessing every perfection of which it has the idea--in other words, all the perfections I conceive to belong to God.

但是，如果它的存在是由于它自身以外的另一个原因，那么，出于同样的理由，我们又要追问这第二个原因是自为存在还是通过别的原因而存在，直到我们从一个阶段到另一个阶段，终于得到一个最终的原因，这个原因就是上帝。


But if it owe its existence to another cause than itself, we demand again, for a similar reason, whether this second cause exists of itself or through some other, until, from stage to stage, we at length arrive at an ultimate cause, which will be God.

------
### 34

显而易见的是，在这个问题上，你不可能有无限的原因，因为所提出的问题与曾经引入我的原因并没有那么大的关系。


34 And it is muite manifest that in this matter thene can he no infinite reoress of causes seeine that the question raised resnects not so much the cause which once nroduced me.

就像我此时此刻所保守的那样。


ias that by which I am at this present moment conserved.

------
### 35.


35.

也不能假定在我的产生过程中有几个原因是同时发生的，也不能假定我从一个原因得到了我认为属于神的一种十全十美的观念，又从另一个原因得到了另一种十全十美的观念，因此，所有这些十全十美确实是在宇宙的某个地方找到的，而不是全部同时存在于一个作为上帝的存在之中;


Nor can it be supposed that several causes concurred in my production, and that from one I received the idea of one of the perfections I attribute to Deity, and from another the idea of some other, and thus that all those perfections are indeed found somewhere in the universe, but do not all exist together in a single being who is God;

因为，恰恰相反。


for, on the contrary.

神的一切属性的统一性、纯洁性或不可分性，是我认为他所具有的主要的优点之一;


the unity, the simplicity, or inseparability of all the properties of Deity, is one of the chief perfections I conceive him to possess;

上帝的一切完美的统一的观念，当然不可能因为任何原因而进入我的头脑，因此我也不可能因此而得到所有其他完美的观念;


and the idea of this unity of all the perfections of Deity could certainly not be put into my mind by any cause from which I did not likewise receive the ideas of all the other perfections;

因为没有一种力量能使我把它们作为一个不可分割的整体来拥抱，而不同时让我知道它们是什么，以及它们以一种特定的方式存在。


for no power could enable me to embrace them in an inseparable unity, without at the same time giving me the knowledge of what they were and of their existence in a particular mode.

------
### 36.


36.

最后，关于我的父母，我似乎是从他们那里生出来的，虽然我所相信的关于他们的一切都是正确的，但就我是一个有思想的存在而言，这并不意味着我是由他们所保守的，甚至也不意味着我是由他们所产生的。


Finally, with regard to my parents from whom it appears I sprung , although all that I believed respecting them be true, it does not, nevertheless, follow that I am conserved by them, or even that I was produced by them, in so far as I am a thinking being.

它们对我的起源所作的一切贡献，充其量只是对我迄今为止所判断的我或我的心灵所包含的事物作出了某些倾向(修正)，而我的心灵，也就是我现在所认为的唯一的我自己;


All that, at the most, they contributed to my origin was the giving of certain dispositions (modifications) to the matter in which I have hitherto judged that I or my mind, which is what alone I now consider to be myself, is inclosed;

因此，关于它们，这里没有什么困难，仅凭这一点就绝对有必要得出这样的结论:我是一个绝对完美的存在，也就是说，上帝的存在，已经被最清楚地证明了。


and thus there can here be no difficulty with respect to them, and it is absolutely necessary to conclude from this alone that I am, and possess the idea of a being absolutely perfect, that is, of God, that his existence is most clearly demonstrated.

------
### 37.


37.

现在只剩下一个疑问:我是如何从上帝那里得到这个想法的;


There remains only the inquiry as to the way in which I received this idea from God;

因为它不是我从感官中提取出来的，甚至也不是像通常的感觉对象的观念那样，当它们被呈现或似乎被呈现给感官的外部器官时，意外地呈现给我的;


for I have not drawn it from the senses, nor is it even presented to me unexpectedly, as is usual with the ideas of sensible objects, when these are presented or appear to be presented to the external organs of the senses;

它甚至不是我头脑的纯粹产物或虚构，因为我没有能力从中提取或添加它;


it is not even a pure production or fiction of my mind, for it is not in my power to take from or add to it;

因此，只有另一种说法，即它是先天的，正如我的观念是先天的一样。


and consequently there but remains the alternative that it is innate, in the same way as is the idea of myself.

------
### 38.


38.

事实上，这并不奇怪，上帝在我被创造的时候，在我体内植入了这种思想，它可以作为工人在他的作品上留下的印记;


And, in truth, it is not to be wondered at that God, at my creation, implanted this idea in me, that it might serve, as it were, for the mark of the workman impressed on his work;

标记也不一定要与作品本身不同;


and it is not also necessary that the mark should be something different from the work itself;

但只考虑,上帝是我的创造者,这是高度之后,他可能在某种程度上塑造我自己的形象和样式,我认为这个相似,其中包含上帝的想法,同样的教员,我理解我自己,换句话说,我让自己反思的对象时,我不仅发现我是一个不完整的,不完美的和依赖,和一个后不断渴望比他更好的服务和更大;


but considering only that God is my creator, it is highly probable that he in some way fashioned me after his own image and likeness, and that I perceive this likeness, in which is contained the idea of God, by the same faculty by which I apprehend myself, in other words, when I make myself the object of reflection, I not only find that I am an incomplete, imperfect and dependent being, and one who unceasingly aspires after something better and greater than he is;

但同时，我也同样确信，我所依赖的那个人，在他自己身上拥有我所渴望的一切财富和我在我头脑中找到的观念，他不仅无限地、潜在地，而且是无限地、实际地，因此他就是上帝。


but, at the same time, I am assured likewise that he upon whom I am dependent possesses in himself all the goods after which I aspire and the ideas of which I find in my mind, and that not merely indefinitely and potentially, but infinitely and actually, and that he is thus God.

和整个力量的观点我有利用自己建立上帝的存在,是由在这方面,我认为我不可能像我这样一个性质的,然而,已经在我的脑海里的想法上帝,如果上帝在现实中不存在——这同样的神,我说,是谁的想法在我的脑海里——也就是说,一个人拥有所有这些崇高完美,心里可能有一些轻微的概念,没有,但是,能够完全理解他们,


And the whole force of the argument of which I have here availed myself to establish the existence of God, consists in this, that I perceive I could not possibly be of such a nature as I am, and yet have in my mind the idea of a God, if God did not in reality exist--this same God, I say, whose idea is in my mind--that is, a being who possesses all those lofty perfections, of which the mind may have some slight conception, without, however, being able fully to comprehend them,

他完全优于一切的缺点，没有一点缺点。这就足以表明他不可能是一个骗子，因为这是自然光的指示，所有的欺骗和欺骗都源于某种缺陷。


and who is wholly superior to all defect and has nothing that marks imperfection: whence it is sufficiently manifest that he cannot be a deceiver, since it is a dictate of the natural light that all fraud and deception spring from some defect.

------
### 39.


39.

但在我更仔细地研究这一点，并考虑其他可能由它演变而来的真理之前。


But before I examine this with more attention, and pass on to the consideration of other truths that may be evolved out of it.

我认为在这里停留一段时间，沉思上帝本人是合适的——这样我就可以悠闲地思考他的奇妙属性——看，钦佩，崇拜这光的美，如此难以形容的伟大，至少，就我的心灵的力量而言，我在某种程度上被这景象所眩眩。


I think it proper to remain here for some time in the contemplation of God himself--that I may ponder at leisure his marvelous attributes-and behold, admire, and adore the beauty of this light so unspeakably great, as far, at least, as the strength of my mind, which is to some degree dazzled by the sight, will permit.

因为正如我们凭信心知道，另一种生命的至高幸福，只在于默想神圣的威严一样，我们现在也从经验中知道，类似默想的冥想，虽然没有那么完美，却是我们今生所能获得的最高满足的源泉。


For just as we learn by faith that the supreme felicity of another life consists in the contemplation of the Divine majesty alone, so even now we learn from experience that a29like meditation, though incomparably less perfect, is the source of the highest satisfaction of which we are susceptible in this life.

## MEDITATION IV

OF TRUTH AND ERROR 
of  truth  and  error
真理与谬误

------
### 1.


1.

在过去的日子里，我已经习惯于把我的思想从感官中分离出来，我准确地观察到，关于物质的物体，我们所知道的非常少，我们对人类的思想，甚至对上帝本身所知道的更多。


I HAVE been habituated these bygone days to detach my mind from the senses, and I have accurately observed that there is exceedingly little which is known with certainty respecting corporeal objects, that we know much more of the human mind, and still more of God himself.

因此，我现在能够毫无困难地将我的头脑从对理智的或可想象的对象的沉思中抽象化，并将其应用于那些脱离一切物质的、纯粹可理解的对象。


I am thus able now without difficulty to abstract my mind from the contemplation of sensible or imaginable objects, and apply it to those which, as disengaged from all matter, are purely intelligible.

当然，我对人类心灵的看法，就它是一种思考的事物而言，它没有延伸到长度、广度和深度上，也不参与任何身体的特性而言，是无可比拟地比任何物质对象的看法更明显的;


And certainly the idea I have of the human mind in so far as it is a thinking thing, and not extended in length, breadth, and depth, and participating in none of the properties of body, is incomparably more distinct than the idea of any corporeal object;

当我认为我怀疑,换句话说,我是一个不完整的和依赖,完整和独立的思想,也就是说上帝,发生在我看来有太多的清洁度和清晰度,从事实本身,这个想法在我,或者,我拥有它存在,上帝存在的结论,我自己的存在,每一刻的延续,是完全依赖于他,是如此的明显,


and when I consider that I doubt, in other words, that I am an incomplete and dependent being, the idea of a complete and independent being, that is to say of God, occurs to my mind with so much clearness and distinctness, and from the fact alone that this idea is found in me, or that I who possess it exist, the conclusions that God exists, and that my own existence, each moment of its continuance, is absolutely dependent upon him, are so manifest,

让我相信人类的头脑不可能知道比这更清楚和确定的事情。


as to lead me to believe it impossible that the human mind can know anything with more clearness and certitude.

现在我似乎发现了一条道路，它将引导我们从对蕴藏着科学和智慧宝藏的真神的沉思，到对宇宙中其他事物的认识。


And now I seem to discover a path that will conduct us from the contemplation of the true God, in whom are contained all the treasures of science and wisdom, to the knowledge of the other things in the universe.

 

------
### 2.


2.

因为，首先，我发现他永远不可能欺骗我，因为在所有的欺诈和欺骗中，都有某种不完善之处。虽然欺骗的能力似乎是狡猾或强大的标志，但意志却毫无疑问地证明了他的恶意和软弱;


For, in the first place, I discover that it is impossible for him ever to deceive me, for in all fraud and deceit there is a certain imperfection: and although it may seem that the ability to deceive is a mark of subtlety or power, yet the will testifies without doubt of malice and weakness;

这样的人，在上帝身上是找不到的。


and such, accordingly, cannot be found in God.

------
### 3.


3.

其次，我意识到我拥有某种判断或辨别真伪的能力，这无疑是我从上帝和其他任何东西那里得到的。


In the next place, I am conscious that I possess a certain faculty of judging or discerning truth from error, which I doubtless received from God, along with whatever else is mine;

既然他不可能有意欺骗我，同样可以肯定的是，他没有给我一种能力，如果我正确地使用它，就永远不会把我引入错误。


and since it is impossible that he should will to deceive me, it is likewise certain that he has not given me a faculty that will ever lead me into error, provided I use it aright.

------
### 4.


4.

在这一点上，毫无疑问，从这一点上说，我永远不会因此而上当受骗。


And there would remain no doubt on this head, did it not seem to follow from this, that I can never therefore be deceived;

因为，如果我所拥有的一切都来自上帝，如果他没有在我身上植入任何欺骗的能力，那么我似乎就永远不会犯错误。


for if all I possess be from God, and if he planted in me no faculty that is deceitful, it seems to follow that I can never fall into error.

因此，当我只想到上帝时(当我认为自己来自上帝时)，并且完全转向上帝时，我就会发现自己没有错误或虚假的原因。但是，随后，我又想起自己，经验使我确信，我仍然受到无数错误的影响。


Accordingly, it is true that when I think only of God (when I look upon myself as coming from God, Fr.), and turn wholly to him, I discover in myself no cause of error or falsity: but immediately thereafter, recurring to myself, experience assures me that I am nevertheless subject to innumerable errors.

当我来调查这些的原因,我注意到,现在不仅是神的意识真正的和积极的想法,或者是非常完美的,但同时,可以说,某种消极的想法,换句话说,在一个无限的距离每一种完美,而我,是与上帝之间,或放置在绝对的存在和不存在之间的这样一种方式,


When I come to inquire into the cause of these, I observe that there is not only present to my consciousness a real and positive idea of God, or of a being supremely perfect, but also, so to speak, a certain negative idea of nothing, in other words, of that which is at an infinite distance from every sort of perfection, and that I am, as it were, a mean between God and nothing, or placed in such a way between absolute existence and non-existence,

事实上，在我里面没有什么使我误入歧途的东西，因为造物我的是一个神;


that there is in truth nothing in me to lead me into error, in31so far as an absolute being is my creator;

但是，另一方面，因为我同样参与某种程度的无或无，换句话说，因为我自己不是最高的存在，因为我缺乏许多完美，这并不奇怪，我应该陷入错误。


but that, on the other hand, as I thus likewise participate in some degree of nothing or of nonbeing, in other words, as I am not myself the supreme Being, and as I am wanting in many perfections, it is not surprising I should fall into error.

因此，我认识到错误，就错误而言，它不是一种依赖于上帝存在的真实的东西，而只是一种缺陷;


And I hence discern that error, so far as error is not something real, which depends for its existence on God, but is simply defect;

因此，为了陷入这种境地，上帝没有必要特意给我这种能力，而是我之所以被欺骗，是因为上帝赋予我辨别真伪的能力并不是无限的。


and therefore that, in order to fall into it, it is not necessary God should have given me a faculty expressly for this end, but that my being deceived arises from the circumstance that the power which God has given me of discerning truth from error is not infinite.

------
### 5.


5.

然而，这还不是很令人满意;


Nevertheless this is not yet quite satisfactory;

因为错误不是一个纯粹的否定;


for error is not a pure negation;

换句话说，这不是简单的缺乏或缺乏一些知识是不应有的，而是缺乏或缺乏一些知识，这似乎是我应该拥有的。


in other words, it is not the simple deficiency or want of some knowledge which is not due, but the privation or want of some knowledge which it would seem I ought to possess.

但是，考虑到上帝的本性，他似乎不可能在他的创造物中植入任何不完美的能力，即由于它而缺乏某种完美的能力。因为，如果这是真的，与造物主的技能成正比，他的工作的完美程度更大，那么宇宙的最高创造者能创造出什么在所有方面都不绝对完美的东西呢?


But, on considering the nature of God, it seems impossible that he should have planted in his creature any faculty not perfect in its kind, that is, wanting in some perfection due to it: for if it be true, that in proportion to the skill of the maker the perfection of his work is greater, what thing can have been produced by the supreme Creator of the universe that is not absolutely perfect in all its parts?

毫无疑问，上帝创造了这样的我，使我永远不会被欺骗;


And assuredly there is no doubt that God could have created me such as that I should never be deceived;

同样地，他总是愿意做最好的事。那么，我能够上当，是不是比我不上当好呢?


it is certain, likewise, that he always wills what is best: is it better, then, that I should be capable of being deceived than that I should not?

------
### 6.


6.

仔细考虑这一点，我首先想到的是，如果我不能总是理解上帝为什么这样做的原因，我一定不会感到惊讶;


Considering this more attentively the first thing that occurs to me is the reflection that I must not be surprised if I am not always capable of comprehending the reasons why God acts as he does;

我也不能怀疑他的存在，因为我发现，也许除了现在，还有其他一些事情，我既不理解为什么也不理解如何被他创造出来;


nor must I doubt of his existence because I find, perhaps, that there are several other things besides the present respecting which I understand neither why nor how they were created by him;

因为，我已经知道我的本性是极其脆弱和有限的，而上帝的本性则是巨大的、不可理解的、我和无限的，因此，我不再有任何困难去分辨，在上帝的力量中有无限的事物，它们的原因超出了我的头脑所能把握的范围。


for, knowing already that my nature is extremely weak and limited, and that the nature of God, on the other hand, is immense, incomprehensible, i and infinite, I have no longer any difficulty in discerning that there is an infinity of things in his power whose causes transcend the grasp of my mind: and this consideration alone is sufficient to convince me, that the whole class of final causes is of no avail in physical or natural things;

因为在我看来，如果不把自己暴露在鲁莽的指责之下，我就不能寻求发现神的不可穿透的目的。


for it appears to me that I cannot, without exposing myself to the charge of temerity, seek to discover the impenetrable ends of Deity.

------
### 7.


7.

我进一步想到，如果我们想要确定神的工作是否完美，就不能只考虑一种造物与其他造物的区别，而应综合考虑所有造物;


It further occurs to me that we must not consider only one creature apart from the others, if we wish to determine the perfection of the works of Deity, but generally all his creatures together;

因为同样的物体，如果它在这个世界上是单独存在的，可能会被认为是非常不完美的，但也可能是最完美的，被认为是整个宇宙的一部分:


for the same object that might perhaps, with some show of reason, be deemed highly imperfect if it were alone in the world, may for all that be the most perfect possible, considered as forming part of the whole universe:

虽然，因为我的目的是怀疑一切，我只是确切地知道我自己的存在和上帝的存在，然而，在注意到上帝的无限力量之后，我不能否认，我们可能已经生产了许多其他的对象，或者至少是他能够生产它们，因此，我可以在他的创造物的一个部分的关系中占有一席之地。


and although, as it was my purpose to doubt of everything, I only as yet know with certainty my own existence and that of God, nevertheless, after having remarked the infinite power of Deity, I cannot deny that we may have produced many other objects, or at least that he is able to produce them, so that I may occupy a place in the relation of a part to the great whole of his creatres.

------
### 8.


8.

因此，更仔细地审视我自己，考虑我的错误是什么(这足以证明我的不完善)，我发现这取决于两个原因的共同作用，即我所拥有的认知能力和选择或自由选择的能力，换句话说，就是理智和意志。


Whereupon, regarding myself more closely, and considering what my errors are (which alone testify to the existence of imperfection in me), I observe that these depend on the concurrence of two causes, viz, the faculty of cognition, which I possess, and that of election or the power of free choice,--in other words, the understanding and the will.

因为仅凭知性，我既不肯定也不否定任何东西，而只是理解(珀西皮奥)我可以形成判断的观念;


For by the understanding alone, I neither affirm nor deny anything but merely apprehend (percipio) the ideas regarding which I may form a judgment;

也没有任何所谓的错误被这样准确地指出来。


nor is any error, properly so called, found in it thus accurately taken.

虽然世界上也许有无数的事物，我在我的理解中都没有概念，但不能因此说我被剥夺了这些概念，就像我的本性所赋予的东西一样，而只是说我不拥有这些概念，因为事实上，没有证据证明神应该赋予我比他实际赋予我的更大的认知能力;


And although there are perhaps innumerable objeets in the world of which I have no idea in my understanding, it cannot, on that account be said that I am deprived of those ideas as of something that is due to my nature, but simply that I do not possess them, because, in truth, there is no ground to prove that Deity ought to have endowed me with a larger faculty of cognition than he has actually bestowed upon me;

无论我认为他是一个多么熟练的工人，因此我没有理由认为他有义务将他所能赋予某些人的完美的东西赋予他的每一件作品。


and however skillful a workman I suppose him to be, I have no reason, on that account, to think that it was obligatory on him to give to each of his works all the perfections he is able to bestow upon some.

此外，我也不能抱怨上帝没有给我选择的自由，没有给我足够丰富和完美的意志，因为事实上，我意识到我的意志是如此的丰富和扩展，超越了一切限制。


Nor, moreover, can I complain that God has not given me freedom of choice, or a will sufficiently ample and perfect, since, in truth, I am conscious of will so ample and extended as to be superior to all limits.

在我看来，非常值得注意的是，在我所拥有的所有其他属性中，没有任何一个是如此伟大和完美，以至于我不清楚地认识到它可能更伟大和更完美。


And what appears to me here to be highly remarkable is that, of all the other properties I possess, there is none so great and perfect as that I do not clearly discern it could be still greater and more perfect.

举个例子，如果我考虑我所拥有的理解能力，我发现它的范围非常小，而且非常有限，同时我形成了另一种具有同样性质的能力的观念，它要丰富得多，甚至是无限的，并且看到我可以构造它的观念，我发现，仅从这个情况来看，它是属于上帝的本性的。


For, to take an example, if I consider the faculty of understanding which I possess, I find that it is of very small extent, and greatly limited, and at the same time I form the idea of another faculty of the same nature, much more ample and even infinite, and seeing that I can frame the idea of it, I discover, from this circumstance alone, that it pertains to the nature of God.

同样地，如果我审视我的记忆或想象能力，或我所拥有的任何其他能力，我发现没有一种能力不是小而局限的，而上帝是巨大而无限的。


In the same way, if I examine the faculty of memory or imagination, or any other faculty I possess, I find none that is not small and circumscribed, and in God immense and infinite.

我所体会到的是如此伟大的意志力或选择的自由，以至于我无法想象还有比这更丰富和更广泛的想法;


It is the faculty of will only, or freedom of choice, which I experience to be so great that I am unable to conceive the idea of another that shall be more ample and extended;

因此，主要是我的意志引导我认识到自己具有某种神的形象和相似之处。


so that it is chiefly my will which leads me to discern that I bear a certain image and similitude of Deity.

因为，尽管上帝的意志能力比我的要大得多，就与之相结合的知识和力量而言，就客体而言，由于上帝的意志能力可以延伸到更多的事物，因此，就意志能力而言，上帝的意志能力比我自己的意志能力要强大得多，但就其本身而言，从形式上和精确的角度来看，我并不认为上帝的意志能力更强:


For although the faculty of will is incomparably greater in God than in myself, as well in respect of the knowledge and power that are conjoined with it, and that render it stronger and more efficacious, as in respect of the object, since in him it extends to a greater number of things, it does not, nevertheless, appear to me greater, considered in itself formally and precisely:

因为意志的力量仅仅在于，我们能够做或不做同样的事情(即肯定或否定，追求或回避)，或者仅仅在于，在肯定或否定，追求或回避理智所建议的事情时，我们的行为使我们意识不到自己被任何外部力量所决定。


for the power of will consists only in this, that we are able to do or not to do the same thing (that is, to affirm or deny, to pursue or shun it), or rather in this alone, that in affirming or denying, pursuing or shunning, what is proposed to us by the understanding, we so act that we are not conscious of being determined to a particular action by any external force.

因为，为了拥有自由，我不必对两个对立面的每一个都同样冷漠;


For, to the possession of freedom, it is not necessary that 1 be alike indifferent toward each of two contraries;

但是，相反地，我越是倾向于一种信仰，不管是因为我清楚地知道在它里面有真理与善的理由，还是因为上帝这样在内心支配我的思想，我就越是自由地选择和拥抱它;


but, on the contrary, the more I am inclined toward the one, whether because I clearly know that in it there is the reason of truth and goodness, or because God thus internally disposes my thought, the more freely do I choose and embrace it;

当然，神圣的恩典和自然的知识，非但没有削弱自由，反而增强和巩固了自由。


and assuredly divine grace and natural knowledge, very far from diminishing liberty, rather augment and fortify it.

但是，当我因为缺乏理由而被迫站在一边而不是另一边时，我所意识到的漠不关心是最低级的自由，它表明了知识的缺陷或否定，而不是意志的完美;


But the indifference of which I am conscious when I am not impelled to one side rather than to another for want of a reason, is the lowest grade of liberty, and manifests defect or negation of knowledge rather than perfection of will;

因为如果我总是清楚地知道什么是真与善，我就永远不会有任何困难来决定我应该作出什么判断，我应该作出什么选择，这样我就会完全自由，而不会永远无动于衷。


for if I always clearly knew what was true and good, I should never have any difficulty in determining what judgment I ought to come to, and what choice I ought to make, and I should thus be entirely free without ever being indifferent.

------
### 9.


9.

然而，从这一切中我发现，我从上帝那里得到的意愿的力量本身并不是我错误的根源，因为这类意愿是极其丰富和完美的;


From all this I discover, however, that neither the power of willing, which I havel received from God, is of itself the source of my errors, for it is exceedingly ample and perfect in its kind;

甚至也没有理解的能力，因为我所设想的一切，除非是上帝赋予我的能力，否则毫无疑问都是我正确地设想出来的，我不可能在这方面上当受骗。


nor even the power of understanding, for as I conceive no object unless by means of the faculty that God bestowed upon me, all that I conceive is doubtless rightly conceived by me, and it is impossible for me to be deceived in it.

那么，我的错误从何而来呢?


, whence, then, spring my errors?

这仅仅是因为，我没有把比知性范围广得多的意志限制在同样的限度内，而是把它扩展到不知性的事物上，因为意志本身对这种事物是有帮助的。


They arise irom this eause aione, that i do not restran the will, which is of much wider range than the understanding, within the same limits, but extend it even to things do not understand, and as the wtll is of ttself mnditterent to such.

它很容易陷入错误和罪恶之中，因为它在真实的空间里选择了虚假，在善良的空间里选择了邪恶。


it readily falls into error and sin by choosing the false in room of the true, and evil instead of good.

------
### 10.


10.

例如，当我最近思考世界上是否真的存在着什么东西时，我发现，因为我思考了这个问题，所以很明显地可以推论出我自己的存在，于是我不得不断定，我如此清楚地设想出来的东西是真实的，这并不是说，我之所以能作出这样的判断，是因为我有强烈的意志倾向，而不是因为任何外在的原因。


For example, when I lately considered whether aught really existed in the world, and found that because I considered this question, it very manifestly followed that I myself existed, I could not but judge that what I so clearly conceived was true, not that I wasl foreed to this judgment by any external cause, but simply because great clearness of the understanding was succeeded by strong inclination in the will;

我越自由、越自然地相信这一点，因为我对它的尊重就越不冷漠。


and I believed this the more freely and spontaneously in proportion as I was less indifferent with respeet to it.

但是现在我不仅知道我的存在,只要我是一个思维,但有同样呈现给我的心灵一定想法的肉体的本性:因此我在怀疑思维自然手语的我,或者说我本人,是不同于物质的性质,还是仅仅是一个和一样的,这里,我想我还不知道任何原因将决定我采用一个相信优先;


But now I not only know that I exist, in so far as I am a thinking being, but there is likewise presented to my mind a certain idea of corporeal nature: hence l am in doubt asl to whether the thinking nature which is in me, or rather which I myself am, is different from that corporeal nature, or whether both are merely one and the same thing, and I here suppose that I am as yet ignorant of any reason that would determine me to adopt the one belief in preference to the other;

我对这两种假设中的哪一种予以肯定或否定，或者我是否在这件事上作出任何判断，对我来说都是完全无关紧要的事情。


whence it happens that it is a matter of perfect indifference to me which of the two suppositions I affirm or deny, or whether I form anyl judgment at all in the matter.

------
### 11.


11.

而且，这种漠然，不仅适用于知性根本不知道的事物，而且一般地也适用于一切知性目前还不能完全清楚地发现的事物。


This indifference, moreover, extends not only to things of which the understandingl has no knowledge at all, but in general also to all those which it does not discover withl perfect clearness at the moment the will is deliberating upon them;

因为，无论那些使我对某一特定事物作出判断的猜想的可能性有多大，只要知道这些仅仅是猜想，而不是确定的、不容置疑的理由，就足以使我得出一个直接相反的结论。


for, however probablel the conjectures may be that dispose me to form a judgment in a partieular matter, the -l simple knowledge that these are merely conjectures, and not certain and indubitablel reasons, is sufficient to lead me to form one that is directly the opposite.

在这一点上，我后来有了丰富的经验。我把以前认为是真的东西都当作假的，只把那些能给我带来一些乐趣的孤独的人放在一边


Of this I latelyl had abundant experience, when I laid aside as false all that I had before held for true, onl the sinele enound that could tn some deeree douht of itl

### 12.


12.

但是，如果我不去判断一件事情，当我没有足够清楚和清楚地构想它时，很明显，我的行为是正确的，没有被欺骗;


But if I abstain from judging of a thing when I do not conceive it with sufficient clearness and distinctness, it is plain that I act rightly, and am not deceived;

但是，如果我决心否认或肯定，那么我就没有正确地运用我的自由意志;


but if I resolve to deny or affirm, I then do not make a right use of my free will;

我若断定是假的，很明显我被欺骗了;


and if I affirm what is false, it is evident that I am deceived;

此外，尽管我是根据真理来判断的，但我是偶然发现真理的，因此我不能逃脱错误利用我的自由的指责;


moreover, even although I judge according to truth, I stumble upon it by chance, and do not therefore escape the imputation of a wrong use of my freedom;

因为根据自然光的规定，对知性的认识应当总是先于对意志的规定。


for it is a dictate of the natural light, that the knowledge of the understanding ought always to precede the determination of the will.

正是这种对意志自由的错误使用，在其中发现了匮乏，构成了错误的形式。


And it is this wrong use of the freedom of the will in which is found the privation that constitutes the form of error.

我说，就我自己而言，在行为中可以发现匮乏，但它并不存在于我从上帝那里得到的能力中，甚至在行为中也不存在，就依赖于上帝而言。


Privation, I say, is found in the act, in so far as it proceeds from myself, but it does not exist in the faculty which I received from God, nor even in the act, in so far as it depends on him.

### 13.


13.

因为我当然没有理由抱怨上帝没有赋予我比他实际赋予我的更大的智慧力量或更完美的自然光，因为不去理解许多事物是有限理解的本性，而被创造出来的理解的本性是有限的;


For I have assuredly no reason to complain that God has not given me a greater power of intelligence or more perfect natural light than he has actually bestowed, since it is of the nature of a finite understanding not to comprehend many things, and of the nature of a created understanding to be finite;

相反，我完全有理由感谢上帝，上帝不欠我任何东西，给了我所有的完美，我决不会认为他不公正地剥夺或保留了他没有赋予我的其他完美。


on the contrary, I have every reason to render thanks to God, who owed me nothing, for having given me all the perfections I possess, and I should be far from thinking that he has unjustly deprived me of, or kept back, the other perfections which he has not bestowed upon me.

------
### 14.


14.

此外，我没有理由抱怨，因为他给了我一个比我所理解的更丰富的意志，因为，由于意志只由一个元素组成，而且是不可分割的，似乎这种能力的性质是，没有任何东西可以从它不摧毁它;


I have no reason, moreover, to complain because he has given me a will more ample than my understanding, since, as the will consists only of a single element, and that indivisible, it would appear that this faculty is of such a nature that nothing could be taken from it without destroying it;

当然，它的范围越广，我就越应该感谢他的恩惠，是他把它赐予了我。


and certainly, the more extensive it is, the more cause I have to thank the goodness of him who bestowed it upon me.

------
### 15.


15.

最后，我也不应该抱怨上帝和我一样，在形成这种意志的行为，或者我被欺骗的判断时，因为这些行为就其依赖上帝而言是完全真实和善良的;


And, finally, I ought not also to complain that God concurs with me in forming the acts of this will, or the judgments in which I am deceived, because those acts are wholly true and good, in so far as they depend on God;

在我的天性中，形成它们的能力是一种比缺乏这种能力更高程度的完美。


and the ability to form them is a higher degree of perfection in my nature than the want of it would be.

关于穷困，只有穷困才构成错误和罪恶的形式原因，这并不需要神的同意，因为它不是一种事物或存在，如果把它作为它的原因而提到上帝，它就不应该被称为穷困，而应该根据这些词在学派中的意义予以否定。


With regard to privation, in which alone consists the formal reason of error and sin, this does not require the concurrence of Deity, because it is not a thing or existence, and if it be referred to God as to its cause, it ought not to be called privation, but negation according to the signification of these words in the schools.

因为事实上，上帝赋予我给予或阻止我同意某些事物的权力，这并不是神的不完美，因为对于某些事物，他没有在我的理解中给予明确而清晰的认识;


For in truth it is no imperfection in Deity that he has accorded to me the power of giving or withholding my assent from certain things of which he has not put a clear and distinct knowledge in my understanding;

但是，毫无疑问，这是我的一个缺陷，我没有运用我的自由权利，乐于对我只是模糊和混乱地设想的事情给出我的判断。


but it is doubtless an imperfection in me that I do not use my freedom aright, and readily give my judgment on matters which I only obscurely and confusedly conceive.

然而，我觉察到，神很容易就这样构成了我，使我永远不会被欺骗，尽管我仍然保持着自由和有限的知识，也就是说，通过在我的理解中植入一种清晰而清晰的知识，关于所有我必须考虑的对象;


I perceive, nevertheless, that it was easy for Deity so to have constituted me as that I should never be deceived, although I still remained free and possessed of a limited knowledge, viz., by implanting in my understanding a clear and distinct knowledge of all the objects respecting which I should ever have to deliberate;

或者只是深深地刻在我的记忆里，不事先有一个清晰的概念就什么都不做判断的决心，使我永远不会忘记它。


or simply by so deeply engraving on my memory the resolution to judge of nothing without previously possessing a clear and distinct conception of it, that I should never forget it.

我很容易理解，如果我把自己看作一个整体，而与宇宙中任何其他的存在无关，如果上帝创造了我，使我超越错误，我就会比现在完美得多;


And I easily understand that, in so far as I consider myself as a single whole, without reference to any other being in the universe, I should have been much more perfect than I now am, had Deity created me superior to error;

但是，我不能因此否认，在宇宙中，它并不是一种更完美的东西，它的某些部分和其他部分一样，都免不了有缺陷，如果它们都是完全相同的。


but I cannot therefore deny that it is not somehow a greater perfection in the universe, that certain of its parts are not exempt from defect, as others are, than if they were all perfectly alike.

我没有权利抱怨，因为把我放在这个世界上的上帝不愿意我保持那种最完美的性格。


And I have no right to complain because God, who placed me inl the world, was not willing that I should sustain that character which of all others is thecheandmostprfect.

------
### 16.


16.

我甚至有理由保持满意的在地面上,如果他没有给我完美的被第一个优于错误意味着我上面已经指出,这取决于一个清晰和明显的知识的所有相关事宜我可以故意,他至少在其他手段,留在我的力量,坚决保留该决议没有法官,真理不是清楚地知道我:


I have even good reason to remain satisfied on the ground that, if he has not given me the perfection of being superior to error by the first means I have pointed out above, which depends on a clear and evident knowledge of all the matters regarding which I can deliberate, he has at least left in my power the other means, which is, firmly to retain the resolution never to judge where the truth is not clearly known to me:

因为，虽然我意识到自己的弱点，不能让自己的心不断地固定在35个相同的思想上，但我仍然可以通过专注和反复的冥想，把它深深地印在我的记忆中，使我永远不会忘记它，当我需要它时，我可以这样做，我可以获得不犯错的习惯。


for, although I am conscious of the weakness of not being able to keep my mind continually fixed on the35same thought, I can nevertheless, by attentive and oft-repeated meditation, impress it so strongly on my memory that I shall never fail to recollect it as often as I require it, and I can acquire in this way the habitude of not erring.

### 17.


17.

由于人类的最高和最完美之处就在于超越错误，我认为，通过今天的沉思，在发现错误和虚假的根源方面，我并没有收获多少。


And since it is in being superior to error that the highest and chief perfection of man consists, I deem that I have not gained little by this day's meditation, in having discovered the source of error and falsity.

当然，这不是别的，正是我现在所解释的。因为只要我把我的意志限制在我知识的范围内，使它除了对那些被知性清楚地表示出来的对象之外，不会形成任何判断，我就永远不会被欺骗;


And certainly this can be no other than what I have now explained: for as often as I so restrain my will within the limits of my knowledge, that it forms no judgment except regarding objects which are clearly and distinctly represented to it by the understanding, I can never be deceived;

因为每一个清晰而清晰的概念无疑都是某种东西，因此它的起源不可能是虚无的，而必然以上帝为它的创造者——我说，上帝，作为一个极其完美的人，不可能毫无矛盾地成为任何错误的原因;


because every clear and distinct conception is doubtless something, and as such cannot owe its origin to nothing, but must of necessity have God for its author-- God, I say, who, as supremely perfect, cannot, without a contradiction, be the cause of any error;

因此，有必要得出这样的结论:每一个这样的概念或判断都是真实的。


and consequently it is necessary to conclude that every such conception or judgment is true.

今天，我不仅学会了我必须避免的错误，而且学会了我必须做的事情，以达到真理的知识;


Nor have I merely learned today what I must avoid to escape error, but also what I must do to arrive at the knowledge of truth;

因为，只要我把注意力充分地集中在我完全想象出来的一切事物上，把这些事物与我想象得比较混乱和模糊的其他事物区分开来，我就一定能达到真理;


for I will assuredly reach truth if I only fix my attention sufficiently on all the things I conceive perfectly, and separate these from others which I conceive more confusedly and obscurely;

为了将来，我将用心留意。


to which for the future I shall give diligent heed.
